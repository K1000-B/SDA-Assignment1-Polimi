\section*{Influence of \texorpdfstring{$\gamma$}{gamma} on the control effectiveness}
\begin{figure}[htbp]
\begin{minipage}[c]{0.48\textwidth}
    \noindent\small
$x_B$ fixed; $E_C=L/L_R$ with $E_C=1$ rigid limit, $E_C>1$ amplification, $E_C<0$ reversal.
All curves start at $E_C=1$. Three regimes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item[$\bullet$] \textbf{Low $\gamma$ ($10^\circ$, $12.5^\circ$):} $E_C>1$ -- elastic twist amplifies aileron effect -- then blocked by divergence; displayed value is actually $q_D$, not $q_C$.
    \item[$\bullet$] \textbf{Intermediate ($15^\circ$):} monotonic decrease, genuine reversal at $q_C=29\,702\ \mathrm{Pa}$.
    \item[$\bullet$] \textbf{High $\gamma$ ($17.5^\circ$, $20^\circ$):} detrimental coupling from low $q$; $q_C=24\,685$ and $21\,427\ \mathrm{Pa}$ -- reversal pressure decreases with $\gamma$.
\end{itemize}
$E_C\sim[\det\mathbf{K}_{\mathrm{TOT}}]^{-1}$; negative branch past divergence is a mathematical artefact. $\gamma$ controls sign and magnitude of aeroelastic feedback: low $\gamma\to$ favorable amplification (divergence governs); high $\gamma\to$ early control reversal.

\section*{Effect of the strut on the reversal dynamic pressure}

\noindent Physical reversal: first genuine zero of $E_C$ with $0<q_R<q_D$; sign changes near divergence singularity are not physical reversals.

\vspace{0.3em}
\noindent

\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{report/img/EC_vs_q_xB_gamma15.png}

\hfill%
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.32\textwidth}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{report/img/qR_qD_vs_xB_gamma15.png}
\centerline{\small\textit{$q_R$, $q_D$ vs.\ $x_B$.}}
\end{minipage}%
\hfill%
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.32\textwidth}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{report/img/qR_map_xB_gamma.png}
\centerline{\small\textit{Map $q_R(x_B,\gamma)$.}}
\end{minipage}

\vspace{0.3em}
\noindent\small
\textbf{$x_B\ge0$:} $E_C$ stays positive up to divergence -- no physical reversal; apparent sign changes near asymptotes are not genuine $q_R$.
\textbf{$x_B<0$:} strut modifies bending--torsion coupling; genuine $q_R$ may appear.
\textbf{Combined $q_R$/$q_D$:} beneficial configuration must raise both thresholds; physical $q_R$ exists only over a limited band of negative $x_B$.
\textbf{Map:} $q_R$ confined near EA/AC for $x_B<0$; increasing $\gamma$ raises $q_R$ -- stronger coupling delays reversal.
\textbf{Design objective:} maximize $q_R$ or suppress reversal within flight envelope while keeping $q_D$ high; strut beneficial only for forward attachment with sufficiently large $\gamma$.


\section*{Convergence analysis of the Ritz approximation}
\noindent
\begin{minipage}[c]{0.28\textwidth}
\small
$q_D^{(N)}$ drops sharply at low $N$ then converges at $q_D\approx8.014\times10^3\ \mathrm{Pa}$; no visible change beyond $N=3$. $\varepsilon_N$ falls by many orders of magnitude, reaching $\sim10^{-14}$ at $N=8$ -- numerical saturation at machine precision, not additional physical accuracy.
